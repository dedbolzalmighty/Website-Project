“URS CAINTA DONATION DRIVE 2021”

Writer: Aileen S. Solayao From: BTLED - 3rd Year

Photojourn: Erica A. Cabuang From: BEED - 2nd Year

Typhoon Ulysses (Vamco), the Philippines' 21st tropical cyclone for 2020, hit Luzon hardest from November 11 to 12. Ulysses unleashed powerful winds and torrential rain that killed dozens of people, destroyed thousands of homes, and left swathes of the island region flooded. Ulysses has become the deadliest tropical cyclone to hit the country this year, so far.

(Typhoon Ulysses: Weather updates, latest news in the Philippines, Rappler, November 25, 2020)

Because of that, many students of URS Cainta had been affected. And so, the University Supreme Student Government together with other Student Organizations of URS Cainta conducted a donation drive to help their fellow students who are in need and also other people who are affected by the Ulysses. Many students gathered their used clothes, bought some canned goods, bottled water and other in kind donations to help. Other students also sent monetary donations, as part of their contribution to the said donation drive.

The University Supreme Student Government and other Student Organizations led by USSG President Jericho T. Mongado together with other officers roamed from Cainta, Pasig, Taytay and Antipolo last November 21, 2020 and collected the donations of the students. They gathered approximately 18 boxes, 11 sacks and 9 plastics/eco bags of used clothes from the students of URS Cainta and other assorted donations like food packs and hygiene kits.

Amidst the pandemic, all of the students showed their willingness to help each other even in small act of kindness for the victims of the typhoon.

Thank you to all students and faculty who contribute for the success of this Donation Drive.

May God bless you all!
Keep safe Giants!

The USSG and other Student Organizations came back to the campus to arrange the donations and sort out the clothes last November 27, 2020. The faculty of the campus also gathered their donations to help.

Wednesday, December 2, 2020, the university picked up the donations in our campus to bring in the places where victims of typhoon are located. In coordination with Prof. Marivic Reyes, the donations were successfully delivered at URS Rodriguez.

“OATH TAKING VIRTUAL CEREMONY 2021”

Writer:Erica A. Cabuang From: BEED - 2nd Year & Jacell Donila From: BSIT - 2nd Year

Photojourn: Aileen S. Solayao From: BTLED - 3rd Year

University of Rizal System Cainta Campus officiated the Oath Taking Ceremony today, November 27, 2020 for the Newly Elected Organization Officers of the school year 2020-2021.

The program started with the prayer followed by the singing of National Anthem and URS Hymn. The welcome remarks was given by Prof. Edgardo Y. Celestial, Campus Director.

He gave an inspiring message to all the organization officers.

"In the midst of a pandemic, we need to help each other; be responsible, and be respectful one”.

This was followed by the Awarding of Certificates to recognize the hard work of the previous Organization Officers of 2019-2020.

The closing remark was led by Prof. Marjorie D.F. San Juan as well. She gave a warm welcome to the new family of USSG Officers who can help and give action to all the students who have problems and needs. They will be the voice of the students in University of Rizal System Cainta. The Masters of Ceremony are Ms. Andrea Jane Salvador and Ms. Beatriz Anne Amparo from BSE-English.

The highlight of the event was the Oath Taking ceremony for all the new officers, led by the Student Organization Unit Adviser, Prof. Marjorie D.F. San Juan.

“STUDENT’S PROGRAM IN RESPONSE TO COVID-19 CHALLENGE”

Writer: Alyssa Klein Coronado From: BSE-ENGLISH - 2nd Year

Photojourn: Nieciel C. Layag From: BSE-ENGLISH - 1st Year

The University of Rizal System Cainta conducted a webinar on Student’s Program in Response to Covid-19 Challenge with the theme, “Online Class Ethics and Discipline.” held on February 6, 2020 at 1:30 pm via Google meet and Fb live.

The program formally started with the prayer followed by the singing of national anthem and URS hymn. The opening remarks was given by Prof. Jonathan Francisco, Dean of Information Technology, he emphasized that “this event might help the students to be more productive and responsible.” In behalf of Prof. Edgardo Y. Celestial, Director of URS Cainta Campus, Prof. Marjorie San Juan the OSDS Coordinator, delivered a message to address the event’s activities “I am thankful because of your coordination and the USSG for having this event.” This issue is a big help for the students as well as the teachers. With that, Andrea Jane A. Salvador and Lyra Mabuti, the Masters of Ceremony welcomed Ariel Salameda the USSG Vice President and he introduced the first speaker Prof. Monette Pilapil.

Prof. Monette Pilapil discussed the Online Class Ethics and Discipline, and opened her presentation with the quote, “Education is not the Filling of a Fail, But the Lighting of a Fire.” According to Prof. Pilapil, “As an educator and future educator in the emotions we feel whether it’s difficult we resist.” Classroom reminders, we need to follow the rules of the class and school. “You need to follow your professor, class reminders and have class etiquette” always remember that. She stated as she ended her message.

Afterwards, Prof. Joy Dee Fernandez URSC Guidance Counsellor, started her presentation with a message “You USSG officers if your classmate has a problem mentally report to me as your guidance counsellor”. As she presented her topic Prof. Fernandez explained the Secrets of Being Mentally Healthy, “We need ways to come up with our problems.” Mental and Emotional Health is our ability to accept our self and others, she added. Prof. Fernandez talked about the importance of emotional and mental health; we’re more confident and comfortable with others. She emphasized “Changes on Behaviour is one of the Cause of Mental Health and Seek for help.” You can come to me as your guidance counsellor.

Moreover, Prof. Marjorie San Juan, OSDS Coordinator lead the awarding of certificate.“If you need help for your mental health, seek for Prof. Joy Dee Fernandez our guidance counsellor, we’re lucky although our campus is small we are not neglected" she stated.

The closing remarks were delivered by Prof. Marjorie San Juan in behalf of Prof. Jameson Martinez Dean, College of Education. “Thank you USSG officers for having this helpful webinar and to our speaker and students.” After this webinar, I’m hoping that students.

have a changes and realization starting on Monday. After that, she welcomed Jericho Mongado USSG President for having his last remark, “Thank you for your participation students of Cainta and for the speakers, Professors and USSG officers.”
Andrea Jane Salvador and Lyra Mabuti served as the masters of ceremony.
